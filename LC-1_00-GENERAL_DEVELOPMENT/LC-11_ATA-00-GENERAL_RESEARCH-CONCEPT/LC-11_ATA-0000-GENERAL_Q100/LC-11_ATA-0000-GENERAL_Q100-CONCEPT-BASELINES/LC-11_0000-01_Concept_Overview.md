## 1. Executive Summary

The AMPEL360 BWB Q100 represents a revolutionary approach to sustainable aviation, combining Blended Wing-Body (BWB) aerodynamics with hydrogen-electric propulsion and AI-orchestrated systems. This document provides a high-level overview of the Q100 aircraft concept, establishing the foundational vision for the research and development program.

### Key Highlights

- **Configuration:** Blended Wing-Body (BWB) with integrated fuselage-wing structure
- **Propulsion:** Hydrogen-electric hybrid system with distributed electric propulsion
- **Capacity:** 100-120 passengers in regional/short-haul configuration
- **Range:** Target 1,500-2,000 nautical miles
- **Emissions:** Zero CO₂ operational emissions (H₂ combustion only produces H₂O)
- **AI Integration:** Neural network-based flight control and systems optimization

---

## 2. Concept Vision

### 2.1 Strategic Objectives

The Q100 concept addresses critical aviation industry challenges:

1. **Environmental Sustainability**
   - Eliminate operational carbon emissions
   - Reduce noise footprint through distributed propulsion
   - Enable circular economy integration (Green-E battery lifecycle)

2. **Operational Efficiency**
   - 30-40% aerodynamic efficiency improvement vs. conventional tube-and-wing
   - Reduced fuel/energy consumption per passenger-kilometer
   - Optimized airport operations and turnaround times

3. **Technological Innovation**
   - Demonstrate hydrogen infrastructure viability
   - Pioneer AI-orchestrated flight systems
   - Advance composite manufacturing for large integrated structures

4. **Market Positioning**
   - Target regional and short-haul routes (500-2,000 nm)
   - Replace aging narrowbody fleet with sustainable alternative
   - Enable new route economics with lower operating costs

### 2.2 Design Philosophy

- **Integrated Architecture:** Maximize synergies between airframe, propulsion, and systems
- **Technology Readiness:** Balance innovation with achievable maturity timelines
- **Safety-First:** Exceed regulatory requirements for novel propulsion and control systems
- **Manufacturability:** Design for efficient composite production and assembly

## 3. Configuration Overview

### 3.1 Blended Wing-Body Architecture

The BWB configuration eliminates the traditional fuselage-wing distinction, creating a single lifting surface that:

- **Reduces wetted area** and drag
- **Increases internal volume** for hydrogen storage and cabin space
- **Distributes loads** more efficiently across the structure
- **Enables distributed propulsion** integration along the trailing edge

### 3.3 Propulsion System Concept

- **Type:** Hydrogen-electric hybrid
- **Architecture:** Distributed electric fans (6-12 units) along trailing edge
- **Primary Energy:** Compressed or liquid hydrogen (LH₂ or GH₂)
- **Secondary Energy:** Green-E battery for takeoff/emergency power
- **Fuel Cells:** PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) fuel cell stacks
- **Backup:** Sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) capable turbines (optional)

## 4. Mission Profile

### 4.1 Design Mission

- **Range:** 1,500 nm with full passenger load
- **Cruise Altitude:** FL350-FL410
- **Cruise Speed:** Mach 0.70-0.75
- **Reserves:** ICAO requirements + 200 nm diversion

### 4.2 Operational Scenarios

1. **Regional Hub-to-Hub:** 500-1,000 nm, high-frequency operations
2. **Transcontinental Short-Haul:** 1,000-1,500 nm, premium routes
3. **Island/Coastal Routes:** Environmental sensitivity zones

---

## 5. Technology Integration

### 5.1 Hydrogen Storage

- **Location:** Distributed tanks within BWB centerbody
- **Technology:** Composite-overwrapped pressure vessels (COPV) or LH₂ cryogenic tanks
- **Capacity:** 1,500-2,500 kg H₂ (mission-dependent)
- **Safety:** Multi-layer leak detection, ventilation, and fire suppression

### 5.2 AI-Orchestrated Systems

- **Flight Control Laws:** Neural network-based adaptive control
- **Energy Management:** Real-time optimization of H₂/battery power split
- **Predictive Maintenance:** AI-driven health monitoring and diagnostics
- **Human-Machine Interface:** AI-assisted pilot decision support

### 5.3 Composite Structures

- **Primary Structure:** Carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP)
- **Manufacturing:** Out-of-autoclave (OOA) processes for large panels
- **Joining:** Advanced bonding and mechanical fastening
- **Inspection:** Embedded sensors for structural health monitoring

## 8. Success Criteria

### 8.1 Technical Performance

- [ ] Achieve 30% reduction in energy per passenger-km vs. A320neo baseline
- [ ] Demonstrate 1,500 nm range with full payload
- [ ] Meet or exceed CS-25 / FAR Part 25 safety requirements
- [ ] Achieve Level 1 handling qualities with AI flight control

### 8.2 Environmental Performance

- [ ] Zero CO₂ operational emissions (H₂ propulsion)
- [ ] 50% reduction in noise footprint vs. conventional aircraft
- [ ] 80% lifecycle carbon reduction (including manufacturing)

### 8.3 Economic Performance

- [ ] 20% reduction in direct operating costs (DOC) vs. A320neo
- [ ] Competitive acquisition cost (target within 15% of conventional)
- [ ] Hydrogen infrastructure compatibility with 2030 airport roadmaps

## 9. Key Assumptions

1. **Hydrogen Availability:** Adequate supply of green hydrogen at target airports by 2030
2. **Fuel Cell Maturity:** PEM fuel cell technology achieves 1.5 kW/kg by 2027
3. **Battery Technology:** 400 Wh/kg energy density for Green-E batteries
4. **Regulatory Framework:** EASA/FAA hydrogen aircraft certification rules available by 2026
5. **Composite Manufacturing:** Scaled production capabilities for large BWB panels
