## Phase 1: Initial Concept Definition (TBD - TBD)

### Overview
Initial exploration of BWB configuration for Q100 regional aircraft with hydrogen-electric propulsion.

### Major Milestones
1. **Project Initiation:** AMPEL360 BWB Q100 concept approved
2. **Initial Studies:** Preliminary sizing and mission analysis
3. **Baseline A Definition:** First concept baseline established

### Key Decisions
- **BWB Configuration Selected:** Trade study between conventional tube-and-wing vs. BWB completed, BWB selected for efficiency and integration benefits
- **H2-Electric Hybrid:** Decision to pursue hydrogen with electric augmentation
- **Regional Mission Focus:** 100-seat class, medium-range regional operations

### Major Changes
- Multiple iterations on planform shape and aspect ratio
- Cabin layout evolved from 2-2-2 to 2-3-2 configuration
- Propulsion location moved from wing-mounted to aft-fuselage integration

### Challenges Encountered
- Center body integration complexity
- Passenger egress and emergency evacuation
- Hydrogen storage volume vs. payload trade-offs

## Phase 2: Refinement and Trade Studies (TBD - TBD)

### Overview
Detailed parametric exploration and formal trade studies to optimize concept and resolve key uncertainties.

### Major Milestones
1. **Parametric Studies Complete:** Wing geometry, mass distribution, energy sizing
2. **MCR-0 Review:** Initial mission concept review successfully completed
3. **Baseline B Definition:** Evolved baseline incorporating study results
4. **Major Trade Studies:** Propulsion architecture, control surfaces, cabin variants

### Key Decisions
- **Propulsion Architecture Finalized:** Distributed electric propulsion with centralized H2 fuel cell
- **Control Strategy:** Combination of elevons and thrust vectoring
- **Cabin Configuration:** 2-3-2 standard with 2-2-2 premium variant option
- **Energy Storage:** 70% H2, 30% battery by energy content

### Major Changes
- Wing span increased by X% for improved L/D
- Fuselage center section lengthened for hydrogen tank optimization
- Vertical stabilizer configuration modified for stability
- Mass breakdown refined with 15% reduction in structural weight estimate

### Challenges Overcome
- Longitudinal stability and control authority validated
- Manufacturing feasibility study confirmed producibility
- Certification pathway identified with regulatory authorities

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well
1. Structured baseline freeze approach provided stability for parallel work
2. Early trade studies resolved major uncertainties efficiently
3. Parametric exploration prevented late-stage surprises
4. Cross-functional reviews improved integration quality

### What Could Be Improved
1. Earlier engagement with manufacturing would reduce iterations
2. More frequent regulatory authority consultations
3. Better integration of systems and airframe teams from start
4. More rigorous change control earlier in concept phase

### Recommendations for Future Programmes
1. Establish baseline discipline from project start
2. Front-load critical trade studies and risk assessment
3. Maintain regular cross-functional integration reviews
4. Document decisions and rationale contemporaneously
5. Plan for concept iteration cycles in schedule
