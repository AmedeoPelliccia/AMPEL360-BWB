# LC-24 Supply Chain & Industrialisation

## Purpose
Manage supplier relationships, procurement, and industrial readiness to ensure successful transition from prototype to serial production.

## Scope

This phase manages the supply chain and industrialization activities required for aircraft production, including:

- **Supplier Technical Packages:** Complete specifications and requirements for suppliers
- **Procurement Specifications:** Contractual and technical specifications for purchased parts
- **Supplier Qualification:** Evaluation and approval of suppliers
- **Industrial Readiness:** Assessment of production system maturity

## Key Activities

### 1. Supplier Management
- Supplier identification and evaluation
- Technical package development
- Contract negotiation and management
- Supplier quality assurance

### 2. Procurement
- Part and material sourcing
- Procurement specification development
- Purchase order management
- Delivery coordination

### 3. Quality Assurance
- Supplier audits and assessments
- First article inspections
- Ongoing quality monitoring
- Non-conformance management

### 4. Industrial Readiness
- Production capacity assessment
- Supply chain risk analysis
- Industrialization planning
- Readiness reviews and gates

## Deliverables

- Supplier technical packages
- Procurement specifications
- Supplier qualification reports
- Quality agreements
- Industrial readiness assessments
- Supply chain risk mitigation plans
- Capacity planning reports

## Tools & Methods

- **Supplier Management Systems:** SAP, Oracle, or similar
- **Quality Management:** AS9100, ISO 9001 compliance
- **Risk Assessment:** Supply chain risk analysis tools
- **Auditing:** NADCAP, AS9102 first article inspection

## Integration

- **Next Phase:** LC-31 Configuration management for production changes
- **Design (LC-14):** Design requirements flow to supplier packages
- **FAL (LC-23):** Supplier deliveries coordinated with production schedule
- **UTCS:** All supplier parts tracked via UTCS identifiers
